## Domain B — half-face at shot start (reframe) — `d25ecf3`

**Symptom (founder):** sometimes shows half a face, especially when a person appears briefly.

**Root cause (reproduced frame-by-frame, not guessed):** `plan_regions` initialised a new fill
region's pan (EMA) from `prev_fill_end_cx` — the *previous* shot's crop centre (commit `ebfc3dc`,
"anti-teleport"). In an interview that cross-cuts between two people, every cut opens the crop on the
*old* speaker's position and slowly drifts to the new one → the new face is half/out of frame at the
start of each cut. Math confirmed it: `0.368 + 0.15·(0.700−0.368) = 0.418` = `prev_cx + smoothing·(face − prev_cx)`.

**Fix:** each fill shot opens on its OWN target (`init_cx=None` → target's first cx). A hard cut
already repaints the whole frame, so the centre snap is invisible; the intra-shot EMA (steady-state
smoothness) is unchanged; region boundaries / frame-grid (Δ=0) untouched.

**Proof (real video, seg 240–270s, 8 consecutive fill shots — gap = |crop-open − face|):**
```
              BEFORE                         AFTER
  r1  traj_start 0.418  gap 0.282  →   traj_start 0.700  gap 0.000
  r2  traj_start 0.488  gap 0.229  →   traj_start 0.717  gap 0.000
  r3  traj_start 0.633  gap 0.219  →   traj_start 0.414  gap 0.000
  r4  traj_start 0.452  gap 0.235  →   traj_start 0.688  gap 0.000
  r5  traj_start 0.629  gap 0.264  →   traj_start 0.365  gap 0.000
  r6  traj_start 0.437  gap 0.257  →   traj_start 0.695  gap 0.000
  r7  traj_start 0.626  gap 0.239  →   traj_start 0.388  gap 0.000
```

## Domain C — hooks rewritten — `8f080a0`

**Problem:** weak/off hooks, sometimes "POV garbage". The old prompt literally listed `pov` as the
first style and capped hooks at ≤6 words (the founder's own model hook is 9 words).

**Fix:** rewrote `prompts/select_moments.v2.txt` + `regenerate_hook.v1.txt` — ground every hook in
WHO speaks + WHAT the clip reveals + SHAPE (personal story vs generalizable insight); styles
`story/insight/question/bold_claim/number` (POV removed); ≤10 words; use the speaker's name from the
title; ban vague "realization/journey/protects" filler; forbid promising a payoff not in the clip.

**Before → after (real transcript, adversarial judges: mean 2.75 → 3.44, POV eliminated, 7/9 accurate, 0 false):**
```

"They called my mental health a..."  →    "Tom Holland: 'I was definitely addicted to alcohol'"
"My struggle to be social sober"     →    "6 months sober: Tom Holland was the happiest he'd been"

```

## Domain D2 — clean clip boundaries (THE critical one) — `67bd5c4`

**Problem (founder):** clips cut mid-sentence, and the END often catches a DIFFERENT person's first
words (a "random outburst" at the end).

**Root cause:** `snap_end_index` reached *forward* up to 8 words for a sentence end, crossing speaker
turns — and the transcript carried **no speaker labels at all** (`diarize=false`).

**Fix:** enabled Deepgram `diarize=true` + added `Word.speaker` (codegen). `snap_end_index` is now
speaker-aware: (0) drop a trailing run by a non-primary speaker, (1-3) never extend the sentence/pause
search across a speaker change, (4) if the primary speaker was interrupted, back up to their last
complete sentence. With no labels it is byte-identical to the old behavior. TDD: 5 new tests.

**Proof (real diarized transcript — 5 of 12 clips had their end corrected):**
- "The truth about Tom Holland's break" (guest = speaker s1): OLD end tacked on **31 trailing words of
  the HOST (s0)** ("Totally. And that's kind of what the message of the show is about… celebrate.").
  NEW end stops at the guest's "…admirable again." right before the host starts.
- "I was addicted to alcohol" (s1): OLD caught 13 words of s0; NEW stops at s1's "…that."
- Every NEW clip ends on `.?!`. The 7 already-clean clips were left untouched (no over-trimming).

**Listen for yourself:** `services/worker/data/th_full/boundary_clips/clip3_OLD_with_outburst.mp4`
(34s, host monologue tacked on) vs `clip3_NEW_clean.mp4` (26.6s, ends on the guest's sentence). Also
`clip1_*` and `clip4_*`. Verify script: `tmp/verify_boundaries.py`.
